Architecture
04 / 10
Why multi-agent?
A single LLM call can't simultaneously assess, plan, teach, and track progress without confusing its own role. Each agent has one job.
Assessment
Diagnose level
→
Curriculum
Build plan
→
Teaching
Deliver lesson
→
Progress
Score & route
The progress agent can loop back to the curriculum agent if level changes — stateful conditional routing via LangGraph.
Agents
05 / 10
The four agents
01
Assessment agent
5 diagnostic questions · scores answers · sets beginner / intermediate / advanced · detects age group
02
Curriculum agent
Selects 3 topics from subject bank · sequences pedagogically · assigns lens rotation
03
Teaching agent
Generates lesson through active lens · adapts vocabulary to age and level · poses check question
04
Progress agent
Scores answer · detects level change · writes next_step to state · LangGraph routes accordingly
Innovation
06 / 10
The lens rotation mechanic
Every topic is taught through three distinct pedagogical lenses. Same content — different relationship with the material.
LENS 01
Neutral & Factual
Who, what, when, where. Foundations. No opinion.
LENS 02
Critical Thinking
Why? What are the different perspectives? What's missing?
LENS 03
Humanitarian
Human impact. Why does this matter to real people?
Responsible AI
07 / 10
Three active guardrails
①
Age-based content filter
Full LLM review for children under 14. Graphic or inappropriate content detected and rewritten. Lighter pass for teens. No filtering for adults.
②
Political neutrality checker
Every geopolitics lesson audited before delivery. Loaded terms reframed as attributed perspectives. Flags logged to server console in real time.
③
Answer safety monitor
Every learner answer scanned before processing. Distress signals and hate speech intercepted. Welfare message shown instead of normal feedback.
